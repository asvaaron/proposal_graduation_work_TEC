\subsection{Main McNemar Results}

Table \ref{tab:mcnemar_overall_pairs} shows the pairwise test for the model with and without head changes for (Base/Small/Large) sizes.  Consider the following null and alternative hypotheses:

\begin{itemize}
\item  $H_0$: $P(A_{correct}, B_{wrong}) = P(A_{wrong}, B_{correct})$ and $[n_{01} = n_{10}]$
\item $H_1$: Models make errors on systematically different samples
\end{itemize}
 
Where: 

\begin{itemize}
\item $n_{01}$: samples misclassified by model A but correctly classified by Model B
\item $n_{10}$: samples correctly classified by Model A but misclassified by Model B
\item $\chi^2=\frac{\left(n_{01}-n_{10}\right)^2}{n_{01}+n_{10}}$
\end{itemize}


\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Overall Accuracy McNemar's Test Results for  Changes vs No Changes in the Classification Heads on RAF-DB}
\label{tab:mcnemar_overall_pairs}
\begin{tabular}{lccccccc}
\hline
\textbf{Model A} & \textbf{Model B} & $\textbf{Acc}_A$ & $\textbf{Acc}_B$ & $n_{01}$ & $n_{10}$ & $\chi^2$ & \textbf{p-value} \\
\hline
Small (No Changes) & Small (Changes) & 0.9237 & 0.9234 & 50 & 49 & 0.0000 & 1.0000 \\
Base (No Changes)  & Base (Changes)  & 0.9276 & 0.9247 & 46 & 37 & 0.7711 & 0.3799 \\
Large (No Changes) & Large (Changes) & 0.9250 & 0.9254 & 43 & 44 & 0.0000 & 1.0000 \\
\end{tabular}
\end{table}


For all model sizes comparisons shown in table \ref{tab:mcnemar_overall_pairs}, the McNemar’s test resulted in p-values greater than $0.05$, indicating that the enhanced classification head does not introduce statistically significant changes in overall error patterns or overfitted results. This confirms that the proposed modifications preserve global predictive behavior.


The similar values of $n_{01}$ and $n_{10}$ across all model sizes suggest that the enhanced classification heads neither systematically improve nor degrade global prediction accuracy. These results confirm that the proposed head modifications preserves the overall error distribution of the models, supporting subsequent analyses focused on class-level performance, macro-averaged F1-score, and efficiency trade-offs.

\subsection{Confusion Matrices}

Figures \ref{fig:cm_base}, \ref{fig:cm_small} and  \ref{fig:cm_large} present a pair of confusion matrices corresponding to the three model configuration sizes evaluated with and without modifications to the head classification module. The matrices are displayed side by side to facilitate direct visual comparison under identical evaluation conditions. 

In all confusion matrices, the vertical axis (y-axis) represents the ground-truth or target class labels, while the horizontal axis (x-axis) corresponds to the predicted class labels. Each cell indicates the number of samples belonging to a given true class that are assigned to a particular predicted class. Correct classifications are represented along the main diagonal of the matrix, where the predicted label matches the true label. Off-diagonal cells correspond to misclassifications, indicating confusion between emotion categories. Emotions mapping abbreviation can be found in this table \ref{tab:emotion_classes}.
